## 🧠 Panic Attack Detection & Classification: Hybrid ANN-RF with XAI
This project focuses on the development of a high-accuracy predictive system designed to detect the occurrence and classify the severity of panic attacks. By leveraging a **Hybrid Artificial Neural Network and Random Forest (ANN-RF)** model, the system combines the deep learning capabilities of ANNs with the robust decision-making of Random Forests, all while ensuring transparency through **Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI)**. 🚀

## 📝 Introduction
Panic attacks are complex physiological and psychological events that often go mismanaged due to a lack of immediate detection. This thesis project aims to bridge that gap by utilizing machine learning to not only identify when a panic attack is occurring but also to categorize its severity (Mild, Moderate, or Severe). A critical component of this research is Explainable AI (XAI), which demystifies the "black box" of the hybrid model, providing clinical insights into which physiological markers most significantly influence the model's predictions. 💡

## 🛠️ Technologies Used
The project was built using a specialized data science stack to handle complex model architecture and interpretability:

* 🐍 **Language:** Python

* 🤖 **Machine Learning:** TensorFlow/Keras (ANN) and Scikit-learn (Random Forest)

* 🔍 **Explainability:** SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) or LIME for XAI integration

* 📊 **Data Processing:** Pandas and NumPy for feature engineering and dataset normalization

* 📉 **Visualization:** Matplotlib and Seaborn for performance metrics and XAI plotting

## ✨ Key Features
* 🧬 **Hybrid Model Architecture:** Integration of Artificial Neural Networks and Random Forests to maximize classification accuracy and F1-scores.

* 📉 **Severity Categorization:** Multi-class classification system that distinguishes between different levels of panic attack intensity.

* 🔎 **XAI Transparency:** Implementation of Explainable AI to provide local and global explanations for individual predictions, identifying key triggers.

* 🛠️ **Preprocessing Pipeline:** Automated data cleaning, feature scaling, and handling of imbalanced datasets typical in medical/health data.

* 📏 **Performance Evaluation:** Comprehensive metrics including Confusion Matrices, ROC-AUC curves, and Precision-Recall analysis.

## ⚙️ The Process
* 📂 **Data Acquisition & Cleaning:** Sourcing and refining physiological data, ensuring high-quality input for the hybrid model.

* 🧪 **Feature Engineering:** Identifying and selecting the most relevant physiological markers (e.g., heart rate, respiratory rate) to enhance predictive power.

* 🏗️ **Hybrid Model Design:** Building the dual-model architecture where ANN and RF outputs are combined to improve overall robustness.

* 🧠 **Training & Optimization:** Utilizing hyperparameter tuning to reduce loss and prevent overfitting.

* 🧐 **XAI Implementation:** Applying interpretability frameworks to explain why the model reached a specific severity classification.

## 🧠 What I Learned
Working on this thesis as a Data Science specialist provided deep technical growth:

* 🔗 **Model Hybridization:** I learned how to effectively combine different machine learning paradigms (Deep Learning vs. Ensemble Learning) to achieve superior results.

* ⚖️ **Handling Sensitive Data:** Gained experience in managing health-related datasets with a focus on data integrity and ethical considerations.

* 🔍 **Interpretability over Accuracy:** I realized that in medical contexts, why a model makes a decision is often as important as the decision itself, leading to my focus on XAI.

* 📈 **Statistical Validation:** Deepened my understanding of Kolmogorov-Smirnov tests and other statistical methods used to validate research findings.

## 🚀 Future Improvements
* ⌚ **Wearable Integration:** Adapting the model to work with real-time data from IoT devices and smartwatches for live monitoring.

* 🌍 **Expanded Diversity:** Training the model on more diverse demographic datasets to reduce bias and improve global generalizability.

* 📱 **Mobile Application:** Developing a user-friendly mobile interface for individuals to track their symptoms and receive model-driven insights.

* 🔊 **Multi-modal Analysis:** Incorporating voice or facial recognition data to complement physiological markers for even higher accuracy.
